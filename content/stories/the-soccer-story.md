**Type:** Sapphic romance — cis/trans, same team, state rec league
**Tone:** Warm, earned, intimate by necessity
**Status:** Concept

## The Shape

Same team, rec league. They've got a physical vocabulary before they ever have a real conversation — they know how the other runs, where she'll be, when she'll cut. One of them has been watching for months. The other has no idea.

## Characters (Working)

- **Midfielder/striker pairing** — or both wings. They share a field rhythm that everyone else can see. The tension is in who notices first and who admits it last.

- **Recently-out trans woman** — this league is the first place she's felt like *herself* on a field. Her teammates are better about it than she expected. She's still learning what it means to be seen.

- **Cis woman, team veteran** — been on this team for years. Hasn't questioned why this particular teammate makes her nervous in a way she doesn't have language for. Not yet.

## Setting

State rec league: shitty fields with patchy grass, carpooling to games three hours away, post-match beers at whatever bar is open, tournaments where you share a motel room because the league budget is nonexistent. Intimate by necessity. An away game in a town that isn't friendly.

## The Turn (Candidates)

- **Injury.** One goes down hard, the other gets there first. The way you grab someone's hand when they're on the ground — that's not a gesture, it's instinct, and that's why it *means* something.

- **Tournament win.** The celebration hug lasts three seconds too long and everyone notices but no one says anything.

- **The away game.** Unfriendly town. Something happens — a comment from the sideline, a look from the ref. One of them handles it. The other sees her differently after.

## What This Isn't

Not a coming-out story. Not a tragedy. Not a lesson. Two women who find each other through something they'd be doing anyway. No grand gesture. Just a rec league season.
