The shell materialized on the planet's surface. All the sensors feeding back data matching the five senses. Runner named Thief. At least that's what the support AI ONI tells her — that her abilities include scanning for loot and a grapple hook, and that she has a contract to investigate a communications relay.

And that's all Thief ever needed to know. The sounds and sights of this foreign moon are all she can truly remember now. In the short few weeks since the arrival, she has been down to the surface she would guess at least a hundred times. She tries to remember about all of the previous encounters and it can't break through. The grass around her sways with a wind that reminds her of somewhere she never went and has not been.

She knows the drill by now. Get in, get the loot, get some kills to brag about, and get out. She rarely goes in with a squad. They tend to slow her down or want to use the loot scanner for themselves, and end up costing her a run and another shell copy — which the cost gets added to her debt. An amount that she knows will never be able to be paid back.

She runs into a former Nu Caloric building. The ground already stained. Tick blood, runner blood — there's very little difference to her. She had recognized a module on one of the corpses while walking by and knew she just had to wait it out. She'd only seen just below these "gold" modules. She didn't understand why they were called gold. Why wouldn't they just be called a different color? Why would they bother to use an element? These were things that she did not know. Even though part of her was screaming that she does.

"Nice grab runner, now upload the tag to the terminal upstairs," ONI says. ONI always says things like this. And things like: "You would live more if you didn't go in solo." And: "New Cascadian systems responding to below surface disturbance." And that disturbance she could feel. Even if she didn't understand what she was feeling, she could feel it — a pulsing, a humming beneath the surface that rode along the data wall she now sits back against. Tucked away in a small corner up on an entrance into a building, away from windows and covered on all sides, she's checking over her inventory.

She hears a noise.

It's not a normal noise.

It's a noise that cuts through. Makes her pause and question what that separate, firm pulse was. And then the sound of metal scraping against rock before a few spurts of gunfire. Then she hears the klaxon, and opens the door, grappling herself into the building. She doesn't tend to stick around when there's trouble. As ONI has told her: she is alone.

She sits at the exfil beacon, panting. She feels the feeling of her shell stretched to its limits. She used her last auto-injector about half a kilometer back, and was now desperately scanning all directions. Watching. She saw a flash run by — without the red telltale glint of those damn battleroids. Spartan-class ones, the most trouble. She heard there were many more variants. ONI told her about them in between runs to the surface. When she wasn't being loaded into a new shell. When she wasn't fragmented across hundreds of CyberAcme servers. When she wasn't lost and finding her way back again.

She shakes her head. Feeling the stiffness, feeling exposed, and hearing gunfire — uncharacteristically not the UESC. Then that pulse again. Not from the planet. From... *that source.* The HUD detects something and she brushes it off. Another glitch. This equipment always glitched. But the mark — it stayed on her HUD. It danced. She wanted to join that dance.

She didn't even know what dancing was. Thief just knew it was something ONI described other runners doing. Or why someone would dance with another. The beacon pulses and a timer starts. The matter transfer. The same sequences as before. ONI starts counting down with her, reminding her about the shell expiration.

10. No — 9 and a half. Where did half a minute go? This made things difficult. And in that split second before it fully took hold, she watched that icon at the edge of her fading vision sway. That she knew. Like a gun sight. Like wind and grass and waves. All the waves.

The runner accelerated herself towards the nearest wall. The metal hit into her as she clambered over. The small railing splattered with a bit of her blood. She knew she had to check this out. She didn't understand.

"Beacon disabled. Moved to new location in 2 minutes," ONI whispers.

She sighed. "I'm going to investigate."

ONI doesn't say anything. ONI doesn't seem to care when she does something stupid. ONI knows that there would be no point to berate a shell before its expiration arrives.

She was now walking around a building. Large — must have been a scientific outpost. Corpo signs. MIDA signs. She left the exfil behind, letting it trigger for no one. The timer read 8 minutes before her natural expiration. Before this run came to an end regardless of her outcome. She knew at this point extraction was very unlikely. Yet she told ONI that she would make it out. ONI knew she was lying.

Thief rounded the corner. Seeing a woman in a hoodie and thigh highs and shorts. Bleeding out. A fellow shell. Not a UESC. Good — enough distance away to take her out with a few shots. To finish her and take that loot marker off of her HUD. To walk up and take a look at whatever she had on her. Her timer read 5 minutes. The last points were spawning in. She could feel it.

She saw this *Vandal.*

ONI informed her it was a Vandal model. A Vandal. Her. *Her* Vandal. She saw the open hand, small fixture inside, and a UESC body flung halfway across a table on the other side of the room.

"Answer me a question. What noise does that make?" Thief says, gesturing down at the device. And she knew Vandal wouldn't respond. A shell can't respond in this state — this state of final moments of self-preservation before expiration.

Vandal coughed. And looked up at her, meeting her eyes. Thief felt a sense of calm? She wasn't sure. And pulled out her knife. And moved closer. She felt, for the first time, *alive.* She felt the steel sliding in past the silkweave skin. She felt complete.

She stood there for a second, wiping the blade clean. Not like it would matter — her new shell won't matter. 2 minutes left. She stood there stunned, some part deep inside screaming to get out. She checked her backpack, her modules. Nothing. She had nothing of note, backing up the assumption it was broken. She didn't even realize as the timer hit 10 seconds. She felt a complete sever from herself, fell back into the abyss.

The last words from ONI being: "Why is it always her?"

Thief had no idea what she meant.
